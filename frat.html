My Kappa Kappa Psi Story


I am a proud member of the Zeta Omicron chapter of Kappa Kappa Psi at the University of Akron in Akron, Ohio. I joined in the spring of 2007 and have been actively involved ever since. I have served as the Vice President for Service, Alumni Relations Officer, and Chapter President. During my undergraduate studies, I also served as North Central District President from 2010-2011, and served as Chair of the Ritual and Regalia Committee in 2009, Chair of the History and Traditions Committee in 2011. It was during this time as an active student that I have the opportunity to travel to several district and national events, meet many Brothers from around the country, and continue to grow as a leader. Many of the relationships that I now have with Brothers started as admiration of their leadership, asking them questions about how they got to where they were, and how I could continue to grow as a leader myself. I am proud to say that many of these Brothers are still my close friends, that I still look up to, and call upon openly when I need advice or support , as our Ritual calls for us to do.

After graduating with degrees in Adolescent Young Adult Social Studies Education and History from UA, I continued to seek out ways to serve both my community and the Fraternity. It was also during this time that I moved to North Carolina to begin my teaching career. The first opportunity to serve Kappa Kappa Psi came in 2013, when I was asked to assist the petitioning group, which at the time was known as a 'colony', as their advisor. During the 2013-2014 academic school year, I served as Advisor to the group at Elizabeth City State University in Elizabeth City, North Carolina, as they were installed as the Nu Iota Chapter. It was during this time I took notes and provided feedback about the process and ways in which I felt it could be improved, such as not relying on neighboring chapters to provide instruction on the Fraternity's lessons or perform the Ritual, as it proved to be an unfair burden to ask chapters who were not immediately nearby.

In 2014, my husband Preston and I moved back home to Ohio, where I continued my career as a social studies teacher at Washington High School in Washington Court House, Ohio. During this time, I also continued to coach high school softball and began coaching girl's soccer, which allowed me to continue to build my leadership skills and work with students outside of the classroom to develop their athletic and leadership skills. While teaching, I also worked in small ways, as many teachers do, to build trusting relationships with my students, and found many to be in want of basic things, such as mending a hole in their clothing, a snack, or simply an adult who would listen. It was from these conversations that I learned that many of my students were struggling with mental health issues, and that they needed someone to talk to, someone who would listen, and someone who would help them find the resources they needed.

In the summer of 2016, National President Jack D. Lee asked me to serve as the North Central District Governor. I was honored to be asked to serve in this capacity, and I took my role very seriously. Applying what I already do in my classroom to the role, I worked hard to build relationships with the chapters in the district and to help them grow and thrive. During this time, along with the other Governors I served with, I worked to revamp the district's website and social media presence, create a more reflective and inclusive process for the district's awards, specifically the Governor's Cup, and continued to work to cultivate the leadership skills of the district officers. It was during my seven year tenure as North Central District Governor that a new position was created on the council, Vice President for Communications, where the Governor's Cup began to include a a rubric that would allow for the district officers themselves to score the top chatpers in our district, and I worked to create workshops that centered on member wellness, mental health, and how to create inclusive, welcoming environments for all. I am proud to call the North Central District home, and to help it grow and thrive.


North Central District Leadership stop for a photo at the National Shrine on Oklahoma State University's Campus while at the 2019 National Leadership Conference in Stillwater, Oklahoma. From left to right: Tammi, NCD President Russell House (Gamma Pi), and Marc Renaud (Zeta Epsilon)

It was in 2019 during this time that I served on the National Curriculum Development Committee, first as a committee member, and then as the Committee Chair for the 2021-2023 biennium. During my time on the committee, a project that I am proud to have initiated was the chapter activity database, as well as two new lessons for the Road to Wisdom that were developed during my time as chair: Our Diversity Matters and Our Sisters Matter. While these lessons were passed along to the National Council, at the time there was not a publications manager at National Headquarters to make final edits. One of the goals I would like to achieve as VPME is to work with the National Council to ensure that these lessons are published and made available to all chapters.

I am also passionate about music and the arts. I have been involved in music for as long as I can remember, whether it was starting band in 5th grade, or singing in the church choice. I believe that music is a powerful tool for bringing people together. In the past, I have been a member of the Medina County Community Band, one of the oldest community bands in the United States, as well as singing in the choir for several years at the Annunciation Greek Orthodox Church in Akron, Ohio. While I am currently in the beautiful chaos that is raising young children, I hope to someday soon join my community band, and continue to grow as a musician. I want to help promote music and the appreciation of music within Kappa Kappa Psi, and I want to push our organization grow and thrive musically in the future.
